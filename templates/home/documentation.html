  1.        Introduction
    1.    Purpose

The Operations Planning System (OPS) represents a groundbreaking leap in streamlining and automating the multifaceted operations of our company. With a focus on efficiency and accuracy, OPS is designed to empower our teams and enhance collaboration by seamlessly linking various operational units. This comprehensive web application serves as a centralized hub, bringing together critical modules to revolutionize the way we plan, execute, and manage our day-to-day activities.

  1.    Scope

In the dynamic landscape of today's fast-paced business environment, the scope of OPS is broad, covering key aspects such as planning, human resources, equipment management, document control, technical office management, engineering management, purchasing, quality control, occupational safety and health, reporting, security records control, and warehouse management. Each module is crafted to address the unique needs of its respective department, contributing to a cohesive and integrated operational ecosystem.

  1.    System Overview

At the heart of OPS is a robust and secure central database hosted on Azure PostgreSQL. This database serves as the backbone, ensuring seamless data flow and accessibility across all modules. The interconnectivity of these modules fosters real-time collaboration, providing a holistic view of operations while allowing for precise control and management. OPS is not just a tool; it's a strategic asset poised to elevate our operational capabilities, drive efficiency, and contribute to the overall success of our organization.

 

  1.        Architecture and Infrastructure

The success of the Operations Planning System (OPS) hinges on its robust architecture and well-defined infrastructure. This section provides a detailed overview of the system's structural components.

  1.    Database
    1. Database Schema

The OPS relies on a centralized Azure PostgreSQL database to store and manage data across all modules. The database schema is meticulously designed to accommodate the diverse needs of each operational unit. Key entities and relationships include:

  • Tables: Representing core data entities such as employees, equipment, documents, and more.
  • Indexes: Ensuring efficient data retrieval and query optimization.
  • Foreign Key Constraints: Enforcing referential integrity between related tables.
  • Views: Offering customized data perspectives for specific reporting requirements.
    1. Data Security

To safeguard sensitive information, OPS implements robust data security measures:

  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC) is enforced to restrict user access based on their roles within the organization.
  • Encryption: Data at rest and in transit is encrypted using industry-standard encryption algorithms to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Audit Trails: Comprehensive audit trails track changes to the database, providing accountability and transparency.
  • Regular Security Audits: Periodic security audits are conducted to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.
    1.    Modules

Each module within OPS is designed as an independent functional unit, addressing specific aspects of operations within the company.

  1. Planning Department
  • Objectives and Functions

The Planning Department module aims to streamline the planning processes, including project scheduling, resource allocation, and task prioritization.

  • Key Features
    • Gantt chart-based project timelines.
    • Resource allocation dashboards.
    • Real-time collaboration tools.
  • Workflow

The workflow begins with project initiation, followed by task assignment, progress tracking, and final project completion.

  1.    User Interface

OPS features an intuitive and user-friendly web-based interface, accessible from various devices and browsers. The user interface includes:

  • Dashboards: Offering a consolidated view of critical information and key performance indicators (KPIs).
  • Forms and Input Screens: Streamlining data entry with well-designed forms tailored to each module's requirements.
  • Navigation Menus: Intuitive menus facilitate easy movement between modules and features.
  • Reporting Interfaces: User-friendly reporting interfaces for generating, customizing, and exporting reports.
    1.    Integration with External Services

OPS is designed to seamlessly integrate with various external services, enhancing its capabilities:

  • Azure Services Integration: Leveraging Azure services for hosting, scalability, and enhanced security.
  • Third-Party APIs: Integration with external APIs for functionalities such as weather updates, supply chain tracking, and more.
    1.    Scalability and Performance

OPS is built with scalability in mind, allowing the system to grow and adapt to the evolving needs of the organization. Performance optimization measures include:

  • Caching Mechanisms: Implementing caching strategies to enhance data retrieval speed.
  • Load Balancing: Distributing incoming network traffic across multiple servers to prevent bottlenecks.
  • Database Indexing: Optimizing database performance through strategic indexing.
    1.    Disaster Recovery and Redundancy

To ensure business continuity, OPS incorporates robust disaster recovery and redundancy measures:

  • Regular Backups: Scheduled backups of the database and system configurations.
  • Failover Systems: Redundant systems are in place to take over in the event of a primary system failure.
  • Geographical Redundancy: Utilizing multiple data centers for geographical redundancy and resilience.
    1.    Compliance and Regulations

OPS adheres to industry-specific regulations and compliance standards:

  • Data Protection: Compliance with data protection regulations, ensuring the privacy and security of user data.
  • Audit Trails: Meeting audit requirements through detailed tracking and reporting of system activities.
  • Accessibility: Conforming to accessibility standards to ensure inclusivity for users with diverse needs.

This concludes the overview of the architecture and infrastructure of the Operations Planning System. The subsequent sections of this documentation will delve into specific details related to each module, user guides, security measures, maintenance, and support.

  1.        User Guide
    1.    Access and Login

To access the Operations Planning System (OPS), follow these steps:

  1. Open a web browser and navigate to the OPS URL provided by your IT department.
  2. Enter your username and password on the login page.
  3. Click the "Login" button to access the OPS dashboard.

Note !!: Ensure that your login credentials are kept confidential. Contact the system administrator for assistance with login issues.

  1.    Navigation

Once logged in, familiarize yourself with the OPS interface:

  1. Dashboard: The home screen provides an overview of the system and quick access to key modules [some features are still under development].
  2. Navigation Menu: Use the menu on the left to navigate between modules. Each module is clearly labeled for easy identification [still under development].
  3. Module Tabs: Within each module, tabs are organized to streamline navigation. Click on the relevant tab to access specific features and information.
  4. Search Functionality: Utilize the search bar to quickly locate specific records or information [still under development].
    1.    User Roles and Permissions

OPS has different user roles with specific permissions:

  1. Administrator: Has access to all modules and can manage user roles and system settings.
  2. Manager: Can access and modify data within specific modules relevant to their department.
  3. User: Has limited access for data input and retrieval based on their role and responsibilities.

Note!!: Check with your system administrator to determine your role and associated permissions.

  1.    Data Input and Management

Follow these steps to input and manage data:

  1. Data Entry Form: Navigate to the relevant module and locate the data entry form.
  2. Required Fields: Fill in the required fields marked with an asterisk (*). These fields must be completed for successful data entry.
  3. Save and Update: Click the "Save" or "Update" button to save your changes. Ensure all changes are saved before navigating away from the page.
  4. Data Validation: OPS may include data validation checks. Review error messages and correct any issues before saving.
    1.    Reporting

Generate reports to analyze and present data:

  1. Reporting Module: Navigate to the "Reporting Program" module.
  2. Select Criteria: Choose the criteria for your report, such as date range, department, or specific parameters.
  3. Generate Report: Click the "Generate Report" button to view or download the report.
  4. Export Options: OPS may provide options to export reports in various formats (e.g., PDF, Excel). Select the desired format.
    1.    Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues while using OPS, follow these troubleshooting steps:

  1. Check Internet Connection: Ensure you have a stable internet connection.
  2. Clear Browser Cache: Clear your browser cache and try logging in again.
  3. Contact Support: If issues persist, contact the OPS support team or your system administrator for assistance.

By following this user guide, you can effectively navigate OPS and leverage its features for improved operational efficiency.

  1.    Human Resources Management
    1. Objectives and Functions

The Human Resources Management module within the Operations Planning System (OPS) is designed to streamline and enhance HR processes within the organization. Key objectives and functions include:

  • Employee Information Management: Maintain a centralized repository of employee records, including personal details, employment history, and performance evaluations.
  • Recruitment and Onboarding: Facilitate the recruitment process, from job posting to candidate selection. Additionally, manage the onboarding process to ensure a smooth transition for new hires.
  • Leave and Attendance Tracking: Enable employees to request and track leaves, while also monitoring attendance to ensure compliance with company policies.
  • Training and Development: Plan, track, and evaluate employee training programs to enhance skills and knowledge within the organization.
  • Performance Appraisal: Automate the performance appraisal process, providing a systematic approach to employee evaluations and goal setting.
    1. Key Features
  • Employee Self-Service Portal: Empower employees to manage their personal information, request leaves, and access relevant HR resources.
  • Recruitment Workflow Automation: Streamline the recruitment workflow, from job requisition to candidate selection, reducing manual intervention and enhancing efficiency.
  • Leave Approval System: Implement a seamless leave approval process, ensuring timely responses and reducing administrative bottlenecks.
  • Training Calendar and Evaluation Tools: Maintain a centralized training calendar, allowing employees to enroll in relevant programs. Evaluate training effectiveness through integrated assessment tools.
  • 360-Degree Performance Feedback: Facilitate a comprehensive performance appraisal system that includes feedback from multiple sources, fostering a holistic view of employee performance.
    1. Workflow

The Human Resources Management module follows a structured workflow to ensure the efficient execution of HR processes:

  1. Employee Information Update:
    • HR personnel update employee records regularly, ensuring accuracy and completeness.
    • Employees can use the self-service portal to update personal information.
  2. Recruitment Process:
    • Job requisitions are raised through the system.
    • Automated posting on relevant platforms and application tracking.
    • Shortlisting and interview scheduling within the system.
  3. Leave Request and Approval:
    • Employees submit leave requests through the system.
    • Supervisors receive notifications for leave requests and can approve or reject within the system.
  4. Training Management:
    • HR plans and schedules training programs using the centralized calendar.
    • Employees enroll in training sessions through the self-service portal.
  5. Performance Appraisal:
    • Goal-setting and mid-year reviews are initiated through the system.
    • 360-degree feedback is collected and consolidated for the annual performance appraisal.

Note!!: By adhering to this workflow, the Human Resources Management module ensures a streamlined and transparent HR operation, contributing to the overall success of the Operations Planning System.

  1.    Equipment Management
    1. Objectives and Functions

The Equipment Management module within the Operations Planning System (OPS) is designed to streamline and optimize the management of all equipment utilized within the organization. The primary objectives and functions of this module are as follows:

  • Asset Tracking: Enable the systematic tracking of all equipment, including their locations, usage history, and status.
  • Maintenance Scheduling: Facilitate the scheduling of routine maintenance activities to ensure the optimal performance and longevity of equipment.
  • Utilization Monitoring: Track the utilization patterns of equipment to identify opportunities for efficiency improvement or potential replacements.
  • Cost Management: Provide tools to manage the costs associated with equipment, including maintenance expenses, depreciation, and potential procurement.
    1.  Key Features

The Equipment Management module incorporates several key features to fulfill its objectives effectively:

  • Asset Registry: Maintain a comprehensive registry of all equipment, including specifications, purchase details, and maintenance records.
  • Maintenance Scheduler: Enable users to schedule routine maintenance tasks, set reminders, and log completed maintenance activities.
  • Utilization Analytics: Generate reports and analytics on equipment utilization, helping management make informed decisions about resource allocation.
  • Cost Tracking: Track and analyze the costs associated with equipment, allowing for better budgeting and financial planning.
    1.  Workflow

The workflow within the Equipment Management module is designed to be intuitive and efficient:

  1. Asset Registration: Users can input details about each piece of equipment, including specifications, purchase date, and initial cost.
  2. Maintenance Scheduling: Schedule routine maintenance tasks based on equipment usage or predefined timelines.
  3. Utilization Monitoring: Regularly analyze utilization reports to identify underutilized or overutilized equipment.
  4. Cost Management: Track and manage costs associated with equipment, including maintenance expenses and depreciation.
  5. Reporting and Analytics: Generate customized reports and analytics to gain insights into equipment performance and make data-driven decisions.

 

  1.    Document Control
    1. Objectives and Functions

The Document Control module in OPS is dedicated to efficiently managing and controlling documents within the organization. The key objectives and functions include:

  • Document Repository: Establish a centralized repository for storing and organizing all types of documents, ensuring easy access and retrieval.
  • Version Control: Implement a versioning system to track changes, updates, and revisions made to documents over time.
  • Access Control: Define and enforce access controls to ensure that only authorized personnel can view, edit, or approve documents.
  • Workflow Automation: Streamline document approval processes and workflows to enhance efficiency and reduce manual interventions.
    1.  Key Features

The Document Control module incorporates essential features to meet its objectives effectively:

  • Centralized Repository: A secure and organized repository for storing documents of various formats, such as text files, spreadsheets, and images.
  • Versioning System: Track changes and maintain a history of document revisions, allowing users to revert to previous versions if necessary.
  • Access Permissions: Set granular access controls to regulate who can view, edit, and approve documents, ensuring data security.
  • Workflow Automation Tools: Automate document approval processes, notifications, and task assignments to streamline collaboration and reduce delays.
    1. Workflow

The workflow within the Document Control module is designed to enhance document management efficiency:

  1. Document Upload: Users can upload documents to the centralized repository, providing necessary metadata and tagging for easy identification.
  2. Version Control: As documents undergo revisions, the system automatically manages versioning, allowing users to track changes over time.
  3. Access Permissions: Administrators can define access permissions based on roles, ensuring that sensitive documents are only accessible to authorized personnel.
  4. Workflow Automation: Automate approval processes, notifications, and task assignments, reducing the time and effort required for document reviews and approvals.
  5. Audit Trail: Maintain a detailed audit trail to track document access, edits, and approvals, ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.
